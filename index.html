About

I operate at the intersection of urban policy and digital innovation. As a Senior Urban Planner at the Department of Human Settlements, I transform complex spatial data into actionable urban solutions. While many planners look at maps, I build the engines that power them.

My work focuses on developing high-impact GIS platforms and mobile ecosystems—like Zhichar, Bhutan's national building registry—that streamline data collection and modernize national infrastructure. I am passionate about leveraging full-stack development to solve real-world logistics, ensuring that Bhutan's growth is data-driven, sustainable, and technologically advanced.

Featured Projects

🏛️ Zhichar — The National Building Registry

The Digital Backbone of Bhutan's Urban Infrastructure

Zhichar isn't just a database; it's a mission-critical GIS platform designed for the Department of Human Settlements. By centralizing national building data into one spatial source of truth, this project modernizes how the country manages its physical landscape. I led the development to ensure that every structure in Bhutan is accounted for, mapped, and ready for future urban growth.

📱 Zhichar Data Collection App

From the Field to the Cloud in Real-Time

Fieldwork used to be a bottleneck; now, it's a breeze. I developed this mobile and web ecosystem to empower surveyors across the country. It allows for high-precision data entry, instant validation, and seamless syncing—even in Bhutan's most remote terrain. This app eliminated manual errors and turned months of data processing into a real-time stream of insights.
